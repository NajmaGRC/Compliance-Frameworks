## 🗂️ Project 1 — Comparative Breach Analysis: Worldcoin Kenya & MGM Resorts

**File:** `Worldcoin-Kenya-vs-MGM-Breach-Analysis.pdf`

A comparative analysis of two cybersecurity incidents from opposite ends of the global compliance spectrum — one rooted in African data privacy law, one in a multinational ransomware attack.

| | Worldcoin Kenya | MGM Resorts International |
|---|---|---|
| **Incident type** | Data privacy breach — unlawful biometric collection | Social engineering → ransomware attack |
| **Regulation** | Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, GDPR | PCI DSS, CCPA, GDPR, Gaming Commission |
| **Detection** | Public outcry → ODPC investigation | Internal detection → self-reported |
| **Outcome** | High Court ordered permanent data erasure (May 2025) | $100M+ losses, no ransom paid, systems restored in 10 days |
| **Root cause** | No DPIA, no informed consent, data sovereignty violations | Social engineering, no MFA, no network segmentation |

**Why this analysis matters:** The Worldcoin case is a landmark in African data protection — 508,569 Kenyan citizens' biometric data collected unlawfully, with socioeconomic factors (poverty, illiteracy) exploited as part of the mechanism. It exposes the gap between policy existence and enforcement in emerging markets, and what it means for organisations operating across jurisdictions.

**Key themes analysed:**
- Gap between regulatory compliance on paper and control enforcement in practice
- Human element as the common thread — from socioeconomic exploitation to social engineering
- Maturity gap in incident reporting: Kenya vs US disclosure frameworks
- Cross-border data sovereignty and DPIA requirements
- Remediation roadmaps: short, medium and long-term controls

**Frameworks applied:** `Kenya Data Protection Act 2019` `GDPR` `PCI DSS` `CCPA` `NIST CSF`

## 🗂️ Project 2 — Compliance Crisis Management: TechFlow Industries

**File:** `TechFlow-Compliance-Crisis-Programme.pdf`

TechFlow Industries — a rapidly scaling fintech with operations across the US, Canada and Mexico — faces simultaneous regulatory crises: a FinCEN notice for AML deficiencies and PCI SSC probation for PCI DSS violations. 90 days to remediate or face $500,000 in penalties, criminal charges and loss of banking licence.

**What was built in response:**

- Compliance programme structure — organisational chart, charter, roles and responsibilities
- AML and PCI DSS policy and procedure documents
- Policy development and approval process with review cycles
- Compliance risk assessment and risk register
- Monitoring, testing and escalation procedures
- Document management system and KPI dashboard design
- Compliance reporting calendar (internal and external)
- 90-day remediation roadmap broken into weekly milestones
- 12-month quarterly compliance plan
- Change management strategy and success metrics

**Frameworks applied:** `AML / FinCEN` `PCI DSS` `Multi-jurisdictional compliance (USA, Canada, Mexico)`
